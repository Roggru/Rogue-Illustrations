Wander

“This is the World,” he said. “Of beasts of burden, of the daemons of dread, gods of glory, and every man to our humankind; just one fiber to a grandeur figure.” Now I know; that these are stories told of tales and myth—linked altogether in one running breath. We share our hate, our spite and tears, our pain and dread. Yet, so too our joy, our contentedness and love all the same. It is through these stories that we are more alike the further we seem to be.

This is the World. “Being” is the common language, and here it will sing:

Good things. Good luck.
